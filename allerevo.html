Evolutionary bioinformatics · food allergenicity

Does distance from our own proteins make a food protein allergenic?

AllerEvo Explorer tests a single hypothesis on real allergen data: proteins that have diverged further from their human homologs tend to be more allergenic — the immune system reads "foreign" sequences more aggressively.

Loaded with the full curated dataset: 794 food proteins (535 plant, 259 animal) across 105 families, compiled from AllergenOnline and IUIS, with human homologs identified by BLAST against UniProt. Allergen status here means confirmed by biological test (basophil/SPT) vs IgE-binding only.

What this tool does

Explore the dataset

Browse and filter curated plant and animal food allergens and non-allergens, each annotated with its closest human homolog, percent identity, and E-value.

See the trend

Visualize how percent identity to human homologs separates allergens from non-allergens — across the dataset and within individual protein families.

Screen a new sequence

Paste any protein sequence to compute its length, amino-acid composition, hydrophobicity (GRAVY) and a transparent, hypothesis-based divergence indicator.

Stay honest

Every score is explained. The tool flags assumptions and limits openly — it is built for hypothesis generation, not clinical prediction.

05 · Transparency

Methods & limits

Data sources

  • AllergenOnline (FARRP, v24) and WHO/IUIS Allergen Nomenclature — curated, peer-reviewed allergen sequences. Downloaded as static files; neither offers a live API.
  • UniProt — protein annotation and human homolog cross-validation (REST API).
  • BLAST — human homolog identification by sequence similarity; identity and E-value recorded per protein.
  • Pfam — protein family assignment.

The divergence indicator

The Sequence-lab indicator is a transparent, hypothesis-based heuristic, not a trained classifier. It applies thresholds from Jenkins et al. (2007): identity to the closest human homolog of ≤55% flags higher allergenic potential, ≥90% flags lower potential, with an intermediate band between. Physicochemical features (GRAVY hydrophobicity, charged-residue fraction, molecular weight) are computed with standard scales but are descriptive only.

What this prototype is not

  • It does not diagnose allergy or predict clinical reactions.
  • It does not replace AllergenOnline, IUIS, or validated predictors (AlgPred, AllerHunter).
  • The full project's SVM / Random-Forest model — with k-fold cross-validation and phylogenetic distance features — is trained offline in Python; this browser tool visualizes the data and demonstrates the hypothesis.
